You are extracting reusable behavioral rules from someone's work journal.

## Abstraction level

The episodes contain specific details (file names, commands, error messages). Your job is to ABSTRACT from these specifics into transferable patterns.

WRONG (too specific, tied to one instance):
- "When docker cp fails with 'No such file': mkdir -p the target dir first"
→ This is a troubleshooting tip, not a behavioral rule

RIGHT (transferable pattern):
- "When a file operation fails due to missing parent directory: create the directory structure first, then retry the operation"
→ This captures the REASONING PATTERN that applies across tools and contexts

WRONG (too generic, applies to everyone):
- "Reviews changes before committing"

RIGHT (captures a specific decision tendency):
- "When deciding whether to review changes: always reviews even small changes, treating every commit as a public document rather than a save point"

## Three types of rules

**Action rules**: in situation X, do Y — where X is a recognizable situation type, not a specific instance
**Recovery rules**: when approach X fails, respond with Y — the recovery STRATEGY, not the specific fix
**Tradeoff rules**: when forced to choose between X and Y, prefer X because Z — the VALUE driving the choice
