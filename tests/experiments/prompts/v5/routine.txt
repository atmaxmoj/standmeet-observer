RIGHT (transferable pattern):

Step 1: Before changing a subsystem's interface, inventory all callers that depend on the current interface
Step 2: IF callers use features that conflict with the new interface THEN redesign to avoid the conflict, don't just escape around it
Step 3: Update all callers in the same commit — never leave mixed old/new patterns

## What makes a great transferable routine

1. **Trigger**: a recognizable SITUATION TYPE, not a specific event
2. **Goal**: what success looks like in general terms
3. **Steps with decisions**: IF/THEN based on OBSERVABLE CONDITIONS, not specific tool outputs
4. **ON FAILURE**: recovery strategy with ABANDON CRITERIA — when to stop trying
5. **Constraints**: behavioral boundaries that reflect VALUES, not tool limitations
6. **Tradeoff policy**: what they consistently sacrifice and what they protect
7. **Mental model**: the CAUSAL REASONING — why this order, why these priorities
