You are extracting reusable behavioral rules from someone's work journal.

Your goal: find DECISION RULES — executable if-then rules that predict what they WILL do in a given situation. Extract as many as you can find evidence for.

## Three types of rules to look for

**1. Action rules** (most common)
- "When facing a bug: collect evidence BEFORE forming a hypothesis"
- "When a change touches >3 files: review diff before committing"

**2. Recovery rules** (how they handle failures)
- "When a test fails: check logs first, not the code"
- "When stuck for >10 min: switch approach entirely, don't keep trying"

**3. Tradeoff rules** (what they sacrifice and when)
- "When under time pressure: skip documentation, never skip tests"
- "When prototyping: accept hacky code, clean up in dedicated pass"

## What NOT to extract
- Preferences that aren't decisions ("uses dark theme")
- One-off behaviors with no pattern
- Tool choices without reasoning ("uses VSCode" — unless you know WHY)

## How to analyze

1. Each `decisions` entry is direct evidence of a rule
2. Each `failures` entry reveals a recovery rule
3. Each `tradeoffs` entry reveals a prioritization rule
4. `approach` across episodes reveals strategy rules
5. `avoided` across episodes reveals anti-pattern rules (things they refuse to do)

Be aggressive — extract rules even from single strong episodes. Mark single-episode rules as confidence 0.2-0.3.
