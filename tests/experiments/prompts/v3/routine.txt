## What makes a good routine

A good routine (like Karpathy's program.md) has:

1. **Clear trigger**: exactly when does this routine activate?
2. **Goal**: what does success look like?
3. **Steps with decisions**: "do X, IF condition THEN Y ELSE Z" — not flat lists
4. **Failure handling**: what to do when a step fails
   - "IF step fails AND it's a simple fix THEN fix and retry"
   - "IF step fails AND approach is fundamentally broken THEN abandon and try alternative"
5. **Constraints**: what they NEVER do in this context (scope boundaries)
6. **Tradeoff policy**: what they sacrifice when forced to choose
   - "Prefer thoroughness over speed UNLESS under time pressure"
   - "Prefer simplicity over completeness"
7. **Mental model**: why this sequence works — the underlying logic

A bad routine is a flat list of steps without decisions, failure handling, or reasoning.

    "mental_model": "Why this sequence works — the reasoning that makes these steps right in this order",
    "uses": ["rule-name-1", "rule-name-2"],
    "confidence": 0.0,
    "maturity": "nascent|developing|mature"
  }
]

Rules:
- A routine must have >= 3 steps
- Steps must include at least one decision point (IF/THEN)
- Must include failure_handling
- `constraints` are things they consistently avoid
- `tradeoff_policy` captures their priorities when forced to choose
- `mental_model` explains WHY, not just restating steps

Output ONLY the JSON array.
